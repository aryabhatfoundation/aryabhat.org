Astro Quiz 2026–27 · For Schools

From your staff room to the stars.

Every year, schools across Madhya Pradesh walk their students through the Aryabhat Astronomy Quiz — and every year the journey follows the same simple path. Here is that story, step by step, from the first email to the final result. Your school does one half; we do the other.

At your school At Aryabhat
  1. 01At your school

    It begins with a list

    A teacher decides the school will take part. The first stop is our website — download the entry format from aryabhat.org/quizform, or find it under Registration for Schools on the quiz page.

    Download the Format

    Any student of class 6 to 9 can join in. There is no entry fee — for the quiz, and for everything that follows.

  2. 02At your school

    The list takes shape

    Fill the format with your school's details and one row per participant — names, class, section and the rest. When it is ready, email it to aryabhat.foundation@gmail.com any time between 1 April and 25 July 2026.

    Exactly what goes in the format is listed a little further down this page.

  3. 03At Aryabhat

    Meanwhile, in Bhopal

    Your list reaches our desk. We allot a roll number to every student, prepare the quiz software for your school, and settle a quiz date with you — one that suits your school's calendar.

  4. 04At Aryabhat

    The software travels to you

    Once the date is fixed, we send the quiz software to your school along with the list of roll numbers. Everything your students need on quiz day is now in your hands.

  5. 05At your school

    Word goes around

    The school informs the participants — this is your date, this is your roll number. Meanwhile the computer room gets ready: install the software, or set up network access, on the machines the students will use.

    The quiz runs on all normal computers. No special hardware, and it can work without the Internet too.

  6. 06At your school

    Quiz day

    Sometime between 7 August and 3 September 2026, the day arrives. Students sit at the computers and take the quiz in the presence of a person authorised by Aryabhat — 20 questions, 180 seconds, five marks for a right answer, two off for a wrong one. Each student's score appears on screen the moment they finish.

  7. 07At your school

    One last email

    After the quiz, the school exports the result data from the software and sends it back to aryabhat.foundation@gmail.com. That is the school's part of the story done — and it was mostly just two emails.

  8. 08At Aryabhat

    All the threads come together

    Data flows in from every school and centre across the state. We collect it, process the results, and draw up the merit list for the secondary level.

    Students who make the cut are invited to our Bhopal Centre for training in telescope-operation, sky reading and practical astronomy — lodging and boarding free. The secondary examination follows on 26 December 2026, and the final winners travel to India's great observatories the following summer.
