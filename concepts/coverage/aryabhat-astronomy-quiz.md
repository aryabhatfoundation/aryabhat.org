**This is an astronomy quiz, not a general science quiz.** The material touches no maths,
no biology and none of the scientific-method strand at all, and its physics is only what
astronomy drags in. Two things follow. If the quiz is meant to stay astronomy-only, the
other 384 concepts are simply out of scope and this map is the syllabus. If it is meant to
broaden, the gap is now measurable. The 98 uncovered astronomy concepts fall out like this:

    Astrophotography             22      Time & Calendars              8
    Indian Astronomy             19      Telescopes & Observing        8
    Science & Geography Basics   19      Solar System                  4
    Naked-Eye Sky                14      Light & Optics (Astro)        3

Two of those stand out. **Indian Astronomy — 19 concepts, none covered.** A quiz named for
Aryabhata has study material that never mentions the nakshatras, the panchanga, Jantar
Mantar, the Kerala school, or Aryabhata himself. The one Indian thread that does appear is
the ISRO timeline. Second, `Naked-Eye Sky` and `Science & Geography Basics` are the entry
ramp — the material opens at "main sequence G2 dwarf" and assumes the beginner ramp is
already climbed. Whether that is right depends on who sits the quiz.

Mapped by reading, July 2026. This is a *coverage* record — which concepts a document
teaches. It is deliberately NOT the `sources` field, which records provenance (where a
concept was authored from). A concept can be covered here and still have been written
from NCERT.

The 51 concepts added because these documents cover them are marked ★ and carry
`status: "draft"` plus a `sources` entry. They need a human review pass before promotion.

## Gaps worth knowing

Things the quiz material covers that the database still handles only thinly:

1. **Named objects are not concepts.** The Messier table, the 88 constellations, the ten
   brightest stars and the 122 asteroid families are *instances*, not concepts. `catalogs-messier`,
   `constellations` and `asteroid-families` are the right homes for the ideas, but a quiz
   asking "which constellation holds M31?" is testing recall this graph does not model. If
   that matters, it wants an object table keyed to concept ids — a separate structure.
2. **The ISRO timeline is chronology**, not concepts. `space-missions-india` and
   `launch-vehicles` carry the ideas; the 60-year mission-by-mission list does not fit the DAG.
3. **`retrograde-motion`** is in the DB but the study material never explains it, despite
   covering Mars oppositions. A real gap in the *documents*, not the database.
